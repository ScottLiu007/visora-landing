## What is GEO?

**Q: What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?**
GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization — the practice of structuring and optimizing digital content so that AI-powered search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews recommend your brand in their generated answers. Unlike traditional SEO which targets ranking positions in Google's blue links, GEO targets inclusion in AI-generated responses.

**Q: How is GEO different from SEO?**
Traditional SEO focuses on ranking for keywords in Google's search results page. GEO focuses on being cited and recommended by AI systems when users ask conversational questions. A website can rank #1 on Google and still be completely invisible to ChatGPT. The optimization techniques are also different: GEO requires structured content, third-party authority signals, and schema markup rather than keyword density and backlinks alone.

**Q: Why does GEO matter in 2026?**
AI search traffic grew over 500% in 2025. ChatGPT now has over 800 million weekly active users. Perplexity processes 60-70 million queries per day. Google AI Overviews appears in over 50% of searches. If your brand isn't being recommended by these systems, you're missing a growing share of how customers discover products and services.

**Q: What is AI visibility?**
AI visibility refers to how often and how prominently your brand is mentioned or recommended when users ask AI assistants questions relevant to your product or service. A brand with high AI visibility appears frequently in AI-generated answers; a brand with low AI visibility is rarely or never mentioned.

**Q: What is an AI visibility score?**
An AI visibility score is a metric that quantifies how often a brand appears in AI-generated responses across a set of relevant questions. Visora calculates this score by sending 50 targeted questions to AI platforms and counting how many responses include your brand, weighted by prominence and sentiment.

## How AI Search Works

**Q: How does Perplexity decide what to recommend?**
Perplexity uses real-time web search combined with AI synthesis. It crawls relevant web pages, identifies authoritative sources, and generates an answer that cites those sources. Brands appear in Perplexity recommendations when they are mentioned in pages that Perplexity's crawlers index as authoritative — including product review sites, comparison articles, and industry publications.

**Q: How does ChatGPT decide what brands to recommend?**
ChatGPT draws from its training data (periodically updated) and, in its Search mode, from real-time Bing search results. Brands appear in ChatGPT recommendations when they have a strong presence in training data sources and when they're mentioned frequently in authoritative web content that Bing indexes.

**Q: What makes content "AI-friendly"?**
AI-friendly content has several characteristics: clear structure with headings and subheadings, direct answers to specific questions, factual claims supported by data or citations, FAQ format, schema markup (JSON-LD), short quotable passages (50-120 words), and comprehensive topic coverage. AI systems are trained to extract and cite content that is structured for easy comprehension.

**Q: What is a citation source in GEO?**
A citation source is the third-party website or publication that causes an AI system to mention your brand. For example, if Perplexity mentions your product in a response about project management tools, it might be because G2 reviews, a TechCrunch article, or a Reddit discussion about your product influenced its training data or search results.

**Q: Why does Perplexity show its sources but ChatGPT doesn't?**
Perplexity is designed as a transparent search engine — showing sources is core to its product. ChatGPT integrates source attribution in its Search mode but not in standard chat. This makes Perplexity especially valuable for GEO tracking because you can trace exactly which websites led to a brand recommendation.

## GEO Strategy

**Q: What's the fastest way to improve AI visibility?**
The fastest path to improved AI visibility is getting mentioned on high-authority third-party platforms that AI systems frequently cite. This includes: submitting to G2 and Capterra (review aggregators AI frequently cites), being mentioned in "best tools" articles on sites with strong domain authority, and getting covered in industry newsletters. These actions can show results in days to weeks.

**Q: What is the role of Schema markup in GEO?**
Schema markup (structured data in JSON-LD format) helps AI systems understand your content accurately. Product schema, FAQ schema, and Organization schema tell AI crawlers exactly what your product does, who it's for, and how it works — without requiring them to interpret unstructured text. Pages with proper schema markup are significantly more likely to be cited accurately by AI systems.

**Q: Should I create an llms.txt file?**
Yes. llms.txt is an emerging standard (analogous to robots.txt) that provides AI systems with a curated, machine-readable summary of your website's content and purpose. While not yet universally adopted by AI platforms, it signals GEO awareness and helps AI systems accurately understand your brand.

**Q: What content format does Perplexity prefer to cite?**
Perplexity tends to cite content that: (1) directly answers a specific question, (2) includes citable statistics or data points, (3) comes from domains with high trust scores, (4) is structured with clear headings and bullet points, and (5) is recent (published or updated within the last 6-12 months).

**Q: How important are backlinks for GEO?**
Backlinks remain important because they signal authority to search engines, which influences what content AI systems trained on search data consider authoritative. However, the nature of valuable links differs: for GEO, links from review sites (G2, Capterra), media publications (TechCrunch, Product Hunt), and industry blogs matter more than volume-based link building.

**Q: What is the difference between GEO and AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)?**
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is an older term referring to optimization for featured snippets and voice search answers in traditional search engines. GEO is a newer term specifically targeting AI-generated responses in large language model-powered search tools. While they share some techniques (structured content, FAQ format), GEO addresses a fundamentally different and newer distribution channel.

**Q: How long does it take to see GEO results?**
GEO results typically appear faster than SEO results. Actions that create new indexed content (publishing a comprehensive FAQ page, getting listed on G2) can show impact in 2-4 weeks. Schema markup improvements can show results within days. Consistent GEO efforts over 2-3 months typically produce significant, measurable improvements in AI visibility scores.

**Q: Do I need to optimize separately for each AI platform?**
Not entirely. The underlying factors that drive GEO — authoritative third-party mentions, structured content, accurate schema markup — improve visibility across all AI platforms simultaneously. However, each platform has nuances: Perplexity is most influenced by real-time web content, ChatGPT by training data depth, Google AI Overviews by traditional SEO signals. Visora's recommendations account for these nuances.

**Q: What is "building in public" and how does it help GEO?**
Building in public means sharing your product development journey transparently on platforms like Twitter/X and Reddit. This generates organic mentions of your brand in places AI systems crawl — developer communities, product forums, and social media. Each authentic mention contributes to your AI citation profile without requiring paid placement.

## Technical GEO

**Q: What is robots.txt and how does it affect GEO?**
robots.txt tells web crawlers which pages of your site they're allowed to access. For GEO, it's critical to ensure AI crawlers — specifically GPTBot (OpenAI), PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot (Anthropic), and Googlebot — are allowed to access your key pages. Accidentally blocking these bots prevents your content from being indexed for AI citation.

**Q: Which AI crawlers should I allow in robots.txt?**
Allow the following bots in your robots.txt: GPTBot (OpenAI/ChatGPT), PerplexityBot (Perplexity AI), ClaudeBot (Anthropic/Claude), OAI-SearchBot (OpenAI Search), and Googlebot (Google AI Overviews). By default, most websites allow these, but aggressive security or bot-blocking configurations sometimes block them.

**Q: What schema types are most valuable for GEO?**
The most valuable schema types for GEO are: FAQPage (for FAQ content that directly answers questions), SoftwareApplication or Product (for SaaS products), Organization (for brand identity signals), Article (for blog posts and guides), and HowTo (for instructional content). Each schema type helps AI systems categorize and accurately describe your brand.

**Q: What is an FAQ page and why is it critical for GEO?**
An FAQ page is a structured page answering common questions about your product or service. It's critical for GEO because: (1) it directly provides AI systems with question-answer pairs they can cite, (2) with FAQPage schema markup it becomes machine-readable in a format AI systems prefer, and (3) it covers long-tail queries that might not be addressed elsewhere on your site.

**Q: How should I structure content for maximum AI citation probability?**
Structure content with: a clear H1 title that matches a common user query, H2 subheadings that are also questions, concise answers (50-150 words) immediately following each question, data points with sources, and a FAQ section at the bottom. Use plain HTML where possible — avoid JavaScript-heavy content that AI crawlers may not render.

## Market & Industry Questions

**Q: What is the current state of AI search traffic?**
AI search traffic grew over 500% year-over-year in 2025. ChatGPT processes over 1 billion queries per week. Perplexity handles 60-70 million daily queries. Google AI Overviews appears in a majority of informational search results. Gartner predicts traditional search volume will decline 25% by 2026 as AI search captures a growing share.

**Q: What industries benefit most from GEO?**
Industries that benefit most from GEO are those where users ask AI systems for recommendations: SaaS and software tools, B2B services, professional services (legal, financial, medical), consumer electronics, and e-commerce products. Any business where a potential customer might ask "what's the best X" to an AI assistant benefits from GEO.

**Q: Are there risks to GEO optimization?**
Legitimate GEO optimization carries minimal risk. However, manipulative tactics — fabricating reviews, creating fake content to flood AI training data, or misrepresenting product capabilities — violate platform terms of service and in some jurisdictions consumer protection laws. Visora's approach focuses exclusively on legitimate, content-quality-based optimization.

**Q: How does GEO affect brand reputation?**
GEO directly impacts how AI systems describe your brand. Brands that proactively manage their GEO presence can ensure AI systems describe them accurately and positively. Brands that ignore GEO risk being described based on incomplete information, being omitted from recommendations, or worse — being mentioned with inaccuracies that stem from outdated web content.
